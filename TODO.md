| ✅ Done | v1.85 Fix Wall Wrap stretching the snake's body across the board | Bug (Steven playtested with a 4-segment snake): the moment a segment wrapped from one edge to the other, the body rendered as a straight line stretched across the whole grid. Two separate causes, both fixed. (1) `updateRenderSnake()` linearly interpolated every segment's render position between its previous and current tick position — for a wrapped segment that's a ~`CELL_COUNT`-cell jump, so it visibly slid across the entire board instead of popping to the far edge; fixed by snapping straight to the final position (no interpolation) whenever a segment's delta exceeds 1 cell. (2) `drawSnakeSmooth()`'s body path was one continuous polyline through every segment — when one segment wraps and its neighbor hasn't yet, they're `CELL_COUNT`-ish cells apart in screen space even though they're still array-adjacent, so the connecting line drew clear across the board; fixed with a new `isWrapSeam()` check that starts a fresh subpath (so each side just ends in its own round cap) instead of connecting across a seam — applied to the shared body path (shadow/outline/base/highlight-stripe all reuse it) and to the scale-arc loop (skips drawing a scale bridge across a seam). Also fixed the same seam case in `tick()`'s per-segment grass-bend loop, which was feeding a huge raw delta into `bendGrassAt()` and would have flung that grass cell's bend wildly. Playwright-verified: forced a 4-segment wrap, captured `renderSnake` mid-tick (head snapped to the far edge, trailing segments still mid-slide on the near edge) and screenshotted it — head renders as a clean separate rounded segment on the wrapped side, no stretch line to the rest of the body. |
| ✅ Done | v1.86 Fix Wall Wrap body flickering after the v1.85 stretch fix | Follow-up bug from v1.85's fix (Steven playtested): the body no longer stretched across the board, but now flickered in what looked like random segments. Root cause: v1.85 snapped only the *individual* wrapped segment straight to its final position, but left it there for the *entire* tick (~100-300ms) while its still-normally-interpolating neighbor kept gliding — so for that whole tick the two segments visibly disagreed, and since a different segment does this each tick as the wrap propagates down the body (one segment per tick), it read as flicker moving around the body. Fixed by snapping the *whole snake* (not just the wrapped segment) to its settled position for any tick where at least one segment wrapped, so every segment stays mutually consistent — the cost is one tick's worth of glide animation for the whole body during that tick, which is an acceptable trade for correctness. Normal smooth interpolation resumes as soon as nothing wraps that tick. `isWrapSeam()`'s path-break logic (v1.85) is still needed and kept — the settled array legitimately has a big screen-space gap between the wrapped and not-yet-wrapped parts of the body while a wrap is straddling the boundary, so the subpath break still prevents a stretched connecting line there; it just no longer has to fight a mismatched neighbor. Playwright-verified: sampled `renderSnake` at 5 points across a single wrapping tick and confirmed it's byte-identical at every sample (no mid-tick disagreement), then captured a real multi-tick 8-segment wrap sequence and confirmed the seam moves cleanly down the body one segment per tick with no extra/spurious gaps. |
